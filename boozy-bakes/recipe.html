Method

  1. 1. Preheat your oven to 170C and line a 12 hole cupcake tray with liners.
  2. 2. In the the bowl of an electric mixer whip the butter and sugar together till light and creamy, around 3-5 minutes. Add the eggs one at a time mixing between each addition. Add half the flour and mix, add the milk followed by the rest of the flour and the baking powder. Mix till combined the mix in the vanilla extract.
  3. 3. Using an ice cream scoop fill each cupcake liner to 2/3 full and bake for 20 minutes until risen and springy when touched. Allow to cool in the tin for 5 minutes before removing to a baking rack to finish cooling.
  4. 4. To make the frosting first mix the butter in the bowl of an electric mixer until pale and soft. Add the icing sugar and 3/4 of the prosecco. Turn the mixer on low and gradually increase the speed. Whip for 5 minutes, if the frosting looks too stiff add a touch more prosecco until you reach a nice piping consistency.
  5. 5. One the cupcakes have cooled slightly brush the tops with the remaining prosecco then take a small knife and hollow out a cone in the center of each cupcake, pop a strawberry in each hole. Fill a piping bag, fitted with an open star nozzle, with the prosecco frosting and pipe a cute swirl on each cake. Sprinkle with the popping candy and pop a little straw in the top.

Ingredients

Cupcake

  • 175g Unsalted butter

  • 175g Caster sugar

  • 3 (large) eggs

  • 175g Self-raising flour

  • 1tsp Baking powder

  • 1tsp Vanilla Extractr

  • 3tbsp Whole milk

  • 12 Strawberries

Frosting

  • 100ml Prosecco

  • 300g Icing sugar

  • 125g Unsalted butter

Method

  1. 1. Preheat oven to 170°C/325°F/Gas Mark 3 and line a muffin tray with 12 cases
  2. 2. Cream the butter and sugar together until pale and fluffy. Add the eggs and beat again.
  3. 3. Add the vanilla extract, lime zest and juice and beat. Don’t worry if the batter splits it will come « Back when you add the flour. Add the mint then 1/3 of the flour and buttermilk, beat together then add the another third then the other, beating in between each addition until just combined and smooth.
  4. 4. Add to the paper cases and bake for 35 minutes. Remove from the oven and cool in the tin for 10 minutes before cooling on a wire rack.
  5. 5. To make the syrup: add all the ingredients to a small pan and simmer until all the sugar has dissolved and leave to cool slightly. When the cupcakes are cool, make holes in the cupcakes by pushing a cocktail stick down into the cakes, don’t push all the way down though as the syrup could make the papers peel. Spoon one teaspoon of syrup onto the holes on each cake.
  6. 6.To make the frosting: add all the ingredients into a large bowl and mix until smooth. Don't overbeat the icing or it will become runny. Add to a large piping bag (no need for a nozzle - just snip off the end if needs be) and pipe a plain swirl on the top. Decorate the cakes with sprigs of mint and lime zest.

Ingredients

Cupcake

  • 170g self-raising flour

  • 20g cornflour

  • 115g unsalted butter

  • 225g golden caster sugar

  • 2 (medium) eggs

  • 120ml buttermilk

  • 2 limes - juice and zest

  • ½tsp vanilla extract

  • 12 large mint leaves, finely chopped

Syrup

  • 50g caster sugar

  • 20ml water

  • 10g unsalted butter

  • 60ml rum

  • 3 mint leaves

Frosting

  • 60g unsalted butter

  • 300g full-fat cream cheese

  • 5tbsp icing sugar

  • 2tbsp lime juice

  • 1 lime's zest

  • 12 mint leaves finely chopped

  • 1tsp of rum
